Approach

My approach is person-centred and integrative. Person-centred means the process is yours, and is guided by you, however you are on the day. Integrative means I work with ideas and techniques from different schools of psychology. My current training integrates systemic and cognitive-behavioural theories, while my introductory studies centred around psychoanalysis and analytical psychology. So my approach starts with a practical attitude towards thoughts, feelings and relationships, acknowledging that problems can have roots in unconscious patterns which might present existential or symbolic aspects.

About

I've worked in design, technology and music for 17 years, as a team lead, mentor, and teacher. My work involves understanding perspectives, guiding conversations, exploring complex problems and supporting peers. I always found my work with people to be the most rewarding, whether helping clients to identify and reach their goals, or helping colleagues to manage stress, complexity and relationships.

My work led me to psychology, philosophy and personal development. Through journalling I became interested in my dreams, which led me to the work of C.G. Jung, and in turn to an interest in psychoanalysis, and depth psychology in general.
